# Starting a Peer Support Group — Checklist

### Phase 1: Foundation
- [ ] **Define your purpose.** What specific need does this group meet?
- [ ] **Choose a format.** In-person, virtual, or hybrid?
- [ ] **Set a schedule.** Weekly? Bi-weekly? Same time every session.
- [ ] **Identify your co-facilitator.** Never facilitate alone.
- [ ] **Draft group agreements.** Confidentiality, respect, attendance, participation.
- [ ] **Find a venue.** Quiet, private, accessible. Or pick a video platform.

### Phase 2: Operations
- [ ] **Create an intake form.** Name, contact, what brings them, any accommodations needed.
- [ ] **Set a max group size.** 6–8 is the sweet spot for peer support.
- [ ] **Establish a waitlist process.** What happens when you're full?
- [ ] **Draft a confidentiality agreement.** Review it at the start of every session.
- [ ] **Decide on record-keeping.** Notes per session? Only if someone is at risk?
- [ ] **Set boundaries.** What happens between sessions? Can participants contact you directly?

### Phase 3: Launch
- [ ] **Recruit your first members.** Start with people you already know.
- [ ] **Do a dry run.** Meet with your co-facilitator and walk through a session.
- [ ] **Prepare an opening script.** Welcome, introductions, group agreements, check-in format.
- [ ] **Prepare a closing ritual.** Summary, resources, next session date.
- [ ] **Send a pre-session email.** Date, time, location/ link, what to expect.

### Phase 4: Sustain
- [ ] **Debrief after each session.** What worked? What didn't?
- [ ] **Track attendance.** Notice patterns and reach out if someone disappears.
- [ ] **Check in with your co-facilitator.** How are they doing? Do you need to adjust roles?
- [ ] **Review group agreements regularly.** Are they still serving the group?
- [ ] **Plan for endings.** What happens when a member leaves? When the group ends?
- [ ] **Evaluate every 6–8 sessions.** Is the group still meeting its purpose?

---

*This checklist draws on guidelines from the International Association of Peer Support Facilitators and years of direct facilitation experience.*
